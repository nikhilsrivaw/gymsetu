COMPANY STRATEGY
How to Build a Vertical SaaS Empire
The playbook for going from one product to a dominant multi-vertical platform company.
01
Master One Vertical First
GymSetu must become the undisputed #1 gym management app in India before you expand. Depth beats breadth at early stage. Every rupee and hour goes into making gym owners say "I can't run my gym without this."
Target: 1000 paying gyms before building Product 02. Cash, credibility, and a reference base — that's your launchpad.
02
Copy the Core, Change the Skin
Your second product (SalonSetu) is 70% the same infrastructure as GymSetu — auth, billing, notifications, AI, Supabase backend. Only the domain logic changes. Competitors start from scratch each time. You don't.
Build a "Setu Core" shared module: auth, payments, notifications, AI layer, white-label engine. All products inherit it.
03
Brand Each Product, One Company
GymSetu, SalonSetu, TiffinSetu — each product has its own brand that speaks to that industry. But all sit under one parent company. Industry-specific branding builds trust. One company builds investor value.
"Setu" means bridge in Sanskrit. You're bridging small businesses to technology — a name that carries meaning at scale.
04
Sell With Stories, Not Features
A gym owner doesn't care about "cloud-based SaaS." They care about "I stopped losing ₹20,000/month because members weren't renewing." Lead with the pain. Show the solution. Prove the ROI.
Create 1 real case study per vertical. Real numbers, real owner, real result. This closes more deals than any feature list.
05
Price for Tier 2/3 India First
The biggest underserved market is not Mumbai or Bangalore — it's Indore, Surat, Coimbatore, Nagpur. Low competition, high SMB density, and owners who pay ₹500–1500/mo without hesitation when shown value.
Freemium is a trap at early stage. Charge from day 1 — even ₹99/mo filters serious customers and validates willingness to pay.
06
AI is Your Moat, Not a Feature
You've already embedded AI deeply into GymSetu. Most vertical SaaS in India has zero AI. Make AI insights so valuable that owners feel blind without them. This is your unfair advantage — double down on it.
Future: a shared AI layer across all Setu products — trained on multi-vertical data. Insights no competitor can replicate.

FOUNDER PRINCIPLES
Rules to Build By
The non-negotiable principles that separate empire builders from single-product founders.
🎯
One Metric That Matters
At any given time, the whole company chases one number. Right now: paying gym customers. Not downloads. Not signups. Paying.
💬
Talk to 10 Customers a Week
Your best product decisions come from owner conversations, not your imagination. Call 10 gym owners every week until you have product-market fit.
🚫
Say No to Scope Creep
Every feature request is a distraction disguised as an opportunity. Build what the top 80% of customers need, not what 1 loud customer demands.
💰
Revenue Before Funding
Build a real business first. ₹10L MRR with no investors is worth more than ₹10Cr raised with no product-market fit. Revenue is leverage.
🔁
Retention Over Acquisition
Getting a new gym is exciting. Keeping 100 gyms paying for 3 years is a business. Obsess over churn. A gym that leaves is a vote against your product.
⚡
Ship Fast, Learn Faster
A perfect product that ships in 6 months loses to a good product that ships today. Ship, learn, iterate. Speed is your only advantage over big companies.
🌐
Build in Public
Document your journey on LinkedIn and Twitter. Founders who build in public get free marketing, investor interest, and customer trust. Your story is your brand.
🤝
Hire for Vertical Expertise
Your SalonSetu sales rep should understand salons. Domain expertise closes deals faster than generic SaaS sales skills. Knowledge is currency.
